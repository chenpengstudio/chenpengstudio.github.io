Chen Peng

Chen Peng / 彭宸 is a Taiwanese artist currently based in Hoboken, NJ. Her work spans painting and ceramics, exploring themes of belonging and healing. Drawing inspiration from nature, animals, and her personal experiences as an immigrant, Peng creates intricate compositions that blend real and imaginary worlds, celebrating the connections we share with our surroundings, nature, and the animals in our lives.

Peng has shown her work in New York, Boston, Cleveland, Columbus, and Taipei, and has participated in artist residencies at the Studios at MASS MoCA, the Vermont Studio Center, and the Wassaic Project. Her work was featured in New American Paintings and Art Maze Magazine, and is included in several public collections, including the Cleveland Institute of Art, Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland Clinic, and Fidelity Corporate Art Collection, among others.

Peng holds an MFA in Painting from Boston University (2022) and a BFA in Painting from Cleveland Institute of Art. Before moving to the US, she received a BA in Philosophy from National Taiwan University in Taipei, Taiwan. She has taught at Cleveland Institute of Art and the University of Akron.
